# What this module covers

Boomi Companion is used in two distinct but complementary ways:

1. [Conversational mode](/concepts/conversational-mode.md) - the developer describes intent, asks questions, and iterates through natural-language prompts.
2. [Canvas mode](/concepts/canvas-mode.md) - the developer opens the integration process Companion has built and inspects, edits, and tests it directly on the Boomi canvas.

Most productive sessions blend the two: developers use conversation to scope and plan, then move to the canvas to verify what Companion produced.

# Why both modes matter

Prompting alone hides what is really happening in the integration. Opening the canvas confirms that map steps, groovy scripts, and connectors are wired the way the developer intends. Conversely, staying on the canvas alone loses the leverage of describing intent in natural language.
