# What this module covers

Boomi Companion, the platform, and the surrounding ecosystem change frequently. This closing module gives developers habits they can adopt so their skills stay current without needing another course every quarter.

# Suggested habits

- Read release notes for the Boomi platform and Companion when they drop.
- Try new connectors in a scratch environment as they enter the [connector registry](/concepts/connector-registry.md).
- Refresh your own reference prompts periodically. What worked six months ago may no longer be the best framing.
- Compare Companion output against your own hand-built patterns. Where they differ, ask whether Companion learned something you should adopt, or made an assumption you should correct.
- Keep a small personal library of "wins" and "friction" notes from the [stopping](/modules/05-stopping.md) practice, and revisit them monthly.

# Why this matters

The gap between developers who use Companion well and those who bounce off it usually is not talent — it is deliberate practice. Habits are what turn a course like this one from a one-time event into a compounding capability.
